Safety & protection

Trust messaging has to be explicit, not implied.

This should feel like a careful marketplace for eligible transfers, not a shady resale bazaar. The language matters as much as the UI.

Use “eligible” more than “guaranteed”

Say that listings are reviewed for transferability signals. Do not imply universal approval.

Avoid unauthorized resale language

Never suggest that any ticket can be flipped. Keep the framing around officially transferable or practically transferable categories.

Explain buyer protection precisely

Use careful wording like “payment held until confirmation flow is complete,” not broad insurance-style claims.

Keep disclaimers visible

Put the rules in the footer and FAQ so they build trust instead of feeling hidden.

FAQ

Questions people will have immediately.

Is this live for every category right now?

No. The launch focus is narrower: events, comedy, concerts, college fests, and selected bus tickets where transfer is supported.

Are train tickets part of the launch wedge?

No. Train tickets are not the primary wedge and should not be the hero use case.

What does “transferable” mean here?

It means the listing appears to fall into a category where official transfer, name change, or a workable operational transfer path may exist. Final eligibility still depends on platform or operator rules.

What does “verified” mean?

It should mean the seller uploaded proof and the listing passed a review flow. It should not imply government certification or blanket legal approval.

How should buyer protection be described?

Keep it concrete: payment, transfer, confirmation, payout. Avoid oversized guarantees that sound like fake insurance.
